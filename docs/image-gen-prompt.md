# Wood Shell Tongue Drum Image-Gen Prompts

Concept visualization only; not fabrication authority. Do not infer tongue
lengths, port dimensions, joinery tolerances, or tuning from these prompts.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished round-body wood shell tongue drum, the recommended first prototype: an ottoman-like black walnut stave cylinder with a flat padauk soundboard, a centered ding tongue and surrounding A Kurd style tongue field cut cleanly into the top, and a discreet gu port visible near the lower side or underside. The instrument should feel premium and handmade, with warm walnut staves, reddish padauk top, a crisp rabbeted rim, satin oil finish, and small rubber feet or a low stand for scale. Clean studio background, soft directional product lighting, 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic scene of the wood shell tongue drum being played in a quiet woodworking studio: the round walnut body sits on a low bench or floor cushion while a player uses soft mallets on the padauk tongue field. Nearby are a tuner, sanding block, wood offcuts, and a small inspection mirror for the gu port, suggesting prototype measurement and tuning without showing labels. Natural window light, warm wood tones, believable hand spacing, calm intimate atmosphere.

## Detail / Macro Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the padauk soundboard tongue field: clean CNC-routed slit tongues with smooth eased edges, a central ding tongue, alternating tongue shapes around the circular top, and the walnut shell rim just visible at the edge. Show fine wood grain, slight end-grain contrast inside the kerfs, a satin finish, and no pitch markings. High-detail macro, shallow depth of field.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, steel handpan dimples, impossible tongue cuts, extra mallets, distorted hands, fake note labels, or cutaway dimensions.
