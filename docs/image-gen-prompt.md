# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished round-body wooden tongue drum standing on a clean neutral studio sweep, photographed at a 3/4 product-photography angle with a ~50mm lens. The body is a turned cylindrical shell with an ottoman silhouette — a stout, low drum roughly the proportions of a small footstool, built from richly figured Black Walnut staves whose vertical grain lines wrap the side wall, finished in a warm hand-rubbed oil-and-wax sheen. The flat circular soundboard cap is a contrasting glowing orange-red Padauk disc, seated flush into the shell rim. The signature feature reads instantly: a field of eleven slim slit-cut tongues routed into the soundboard, one larger central ding tongue surrounded by ten progressively shorter tongues fanned outward left and right in a zigzag-ascending arrangement, each tongue a clean narrow kerf in the wood face. A pair of soft mallets rests beside it for scale, conveying a tabletop / lap-sized instrument. Soft directional studio light rakes gently across the soundboard to catch the tongue slits and the wood grain. High detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same round walnut-and-Padauk tongue drum staged in a warm woodworker's studio, sitting on a workbench among soft natural window light, with a band-clamped stave glue-up, a lathe, and curls of shavings softly blurred in the background. A player's hands hover with mallets just above the orange-red Padauk soundboard, mid-strike on the tongue field, suggesting a calm seated lap-play session. Shallow depth of field isolates the drum, environmental storytelling of a small one-maker shop, golden ambient light. Concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the soundboard tongue field: the glowing orange-red Padauk top filling the frame, showing the narrow CNC-routed slit kerfs that define the cantilever tongues, the rounded slit-terminus relief at each tongue base, and the tight grain of the tonewood. The central ding tongue and the inner edges of the neighboring fanned tongues are visible, with the flush rabbet seam where the Padauk cap meets the dark walnut shell rim along one side. Raking side light skims across the surface to throw the slit edges into crisp relief and reveal fine surface texture. High detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms, metal tongues, or steel hand-pan dimpling; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
