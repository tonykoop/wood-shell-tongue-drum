# Notes: Simple Gifts (Wood-Shell Tongue Drum)

**Provenance:** Elder Joseph Brackett, Shaker hymn (1848). PD. 
Copland's arrangement is NOT used.

**Range check:** E4–D5. The wood drum's warm tone suits the flowing, arching phrase shape.

**Practice tips:**
1. The wood shell creates a shorter sustain than steel — use clean attacks to compensate.
2. The arch shape (ascending then descending) benefits from slight crescendo going up,
   diminuendo coming down.
3. The Bb (b in K:F) will sound especially warm through the wood vessel.
