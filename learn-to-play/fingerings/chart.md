# Wood-Shell Tongue Drum Layout Reference

The tongue fields are arranged in a circular pattern on the drum head.
The exact layout depends on the builder's configuration, but a typical arrangement:

```
Standard 16" (A3 ding) suggested layout:

        Top (highest fields)
            ┌─────────────┐
          A4            G4
        ┌──────────────────┐
       F4                 E4
        └──────────────────┘
          D4            C4
            └─────────────┘
              Bb3   B3
                ┌─────┐
                │ A3  │  ← DING (center, bottom dome)
                └─────┘

Travel (12", D4 ding): All fields shifted up a fourth
Floor Pouf (20", D3 ding): All fields shifted down an octave
```

## Playing technique (wood vs. steel)
- **Softer mallets work better** on wood than on steel — the wood absorbs harder impacts
  without producing the bright ring of steel.
- **Lower striking force** needed — wood resonates more readily than steel drums.
- **Shorter sustain** — wood damps faster; play each note at the moment you want it heard.
- The ding dome (center) is struck more gently than the tongue fields — a glancing blow
  rather than a full mallet stroke.
