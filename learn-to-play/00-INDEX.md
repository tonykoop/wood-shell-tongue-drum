# Wood-Shell Tongue Drum — Starter Songbook

Your wood-shell tongue drum uses a **Ding** center note (D) with tongue fields arranged
around a resonating wood vessel. Size variants:
- **Travel** (12" OD): D4 ding
- **Standard** (16" OD): A3 ding  
- **Floor Pouf** (20" OD): D3 ding

This songbook is written for the **Standard** tuning (A3 ding). If you built a different
size, transpose the exercises to match your ding note.

## Recommended scale (D natural minor, A3 ding)
A3 — B3 — C4 — D4 — E4 — F4 — G4 — A4 — and the D ding

The tongue fields follow a D natural minor (Aeolian) arrangement, providing
a rich, warm tone with the wooden shell adding natural warmth.

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: ding + nearby fields, slow patterns
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: cross-tongue patterns, wider range
- ★★★ — Advanced: full range, complex rhythmic patterns

## Wood tone note
The wood shell produces a warmer, more muted tone than steel tongue drums.
Let each note bloom fully before moving to the next — the sustain is shorter
than steel, so clean attacks matter more.
